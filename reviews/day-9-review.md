# Day 9 Review — Content Expansion & Timer

## Review 1: Gameplay Focus

The 5 new levels are a significant content boost. Going from 10 to 15 levels adds ~50% more gameplay. Chapter 3 (Multi-Output) introduces genuinely new concepts:
- **Half Adder** (L11): First dual-output level. Elegant — XOR for SUM, AND for CARRY. Teaches that real computing circuits have multiple outputs.
- **3-Input AND** (L12): Teaches gate chaining. Simple but foundational.
- **Majority Vote** (L13): Complex! Needs 5 gates (3 ANDs + 2 ORs). Real computer science concept.
- **Multiplexer** (L14): Brilliant level design — selector switches between inputs. Core networking/CPU concept.
- **Full Adder** (L15): Boss-level difficulty. 5 gates, 3 inputs, 2 outputs. Satisfying to complete.

The timer adds a speedrun dimension. After completing a level, you naturally want to try faster. The ⏱ display in the status bar is unobtrusive.

The audio improvements are subtle but positive. The click has more "snap" to it, the zap is buzzier, the success chord is richer. Still not amazing but better.

## Review 2: Design Focus

The level progression from Chapter 1 (single gates) → Chapter 2 (compositions) → Chapter 3 (multi-output/3-input) creates a natural learning curve. Each chapter introduces a new dimension of complexity.

Level 15 (Full Adder) is a perfect capstone. It combines everything learned: XOR for arithmetic, AND for carry detection, OR for combining carry sources. This is genuinely educational — someone playing through all 15 levels learns real digital logic.

The timer is well-placed but the best-time display on level select cards is missing (it's only in progress data, not shown). That would add replay motivation.

The truth table for multi-output levels (4+ columns) can be wide. On mobile, this could overflow. Worth testing.

## Review 3: Fresh Eyes

15 levels! Three chapters! This feels like a real game now. The level select screen with three chapter sections is organized and inviting. Each chapter clearly builds on the previous.

Playing Level 11 (Half Adder) — this is where it gets interesting. Two output nodes! I need to figure out both SUM and CARRY. The hint says "XOR for SUM, AND for CARRY." That's elegant and teaches something real about how computers work.

The timer in the corner creates gentle time pressure without being stressful. It's there if you want it, ignorable if you don't.

The game now has enough content for a satisfying session. 15 puzzle levels + random challenges + sandbox mode = plenty to do. What would push it further: achievements/badges, a completion percentage display, or unlockable cosmetic themes.

### Top Priority for Tomorrow
**Visual Design upgrade.** Visual Design has been stuck at 7/10 throughout. The game needs: a refined color palette, better gate rendering (perhaps with pin diagrams), improved level card designs, and a more polished overall look. The breadboard aesthetic is good but could be elevated.

### Secondary Priorities

2. Completion percentage / progress indicator
3. Achievement badges for milestones
